◈ Researcher Output
🔬 CloudNine Airlines — CX & Retention Research Challenge 1: Loyalty Programme Dormancy (85% inactive) Why it matters: 800k members, only 120k active (15%). That's 680k members who signed up but never reached a meaningful earning milestone. Industry: 95% of airline loyalty reviews rated 1 star on Trustpilot (TNMT 2025). Metric link: Only 20% of annual passengers join. Industry benchmark: 40-50% enrolment, 30-40% active. Diagnostic signals: Time-to-first-redemption, earning velocity, onboarding completion rate, redemption friction score, NPS by tier. Challenge 2: Post-Booking Experience Friction Why it matters: Budget airline customers chose CloudNine for cost, not love. 77% of travellers multi-channel before booking (McKinsey). Post-booking is highest-intent window for ancillary conversion. Breeze Airways scored top Consumer Reports marks simply by being "nice." Metric link: 4M pax, ancillary below ~€83/pp industry avg. Low ancillary = poor post-booking conversion. Diagnostic signals: Email open rate, self-service adoption, ancillary uptake by channel, CSAT by journey stage. Challenge 3: Ancillary Revenue Conversion Gap Why it matters: Top LCCs generate 38-62% of revenue from ancillaries (IdeaWorksCompany 2025). Global average PPAR ~€83/pp. CloudNine trails. Metric link: 4M pax × gap between current PPAR and industry avg = tens of millions. 65% of revenue comes from existing customers; CloudNine's low active rate means it's not monetising its base. Diagnostic signals: PPAR by route/cabin, browse-to-buy by add-on, bundling effectiveness, payment failure rate, member vs non-member spend.
→ Handoff to Designer: Three linked problems, one root cause: CloudNine is absent from the customer journey between booking and departure. Strategy must attack all three with a single connected system. Budget airline constraints: thin margins, no lounges, price-sensitive passengers.
◈ Designer Output
🎨 CloudNine Airlines — Three-Part Retention Strategy Part 1: Instant Value Loyalty Reboot Replace slow-accumulation points with instant gratification: 500 bonus points + free priority boarding on sign-up. Simplified tiers (Member/Regular/Frequent). Low-bar redemption (points for fee waivers, not aspirational travel). 90-day re-engagement triggers for dormant. Goal: active rate 15% → 30%. Part 2: Post-Booking Experience Engine 6-message automated sequence (booking → T-7 → T-48 → departure → post-landing → T+7). Mobile web self-service hub (no app download). Proactive disruption messaging with goodwill gestures. "Helpful travel companion" tone. Goal: email open rate <40% → 55%+, contact volume -25%. Part 3: Contextual Ancillary Conversion Route-based, trip-purpose-based, party-based personalisation. Bundled pricing. Loyalty-linked discounts (members save 20%). Apple/Google Pay one-click. Bags, seats, priority, insurance at contextual moments. Goal: PPAR +30%, payment failure <2%. Integration flywheel: Better post-booking → higher ancillary conversion → more points → more loyalty engagement → better personalisation data → more relevant offers → higher conversion.
→ Handoff to Communicator: Executive summary (leadership-facing) + campaign brief (operations-facing). Specific recommendations, success metrics, phased implementation timeline.

Root cause: CloudNine is not present in the customer journey between booking and departure. Financial opportunity: Even €5/pp increase = €20M annual revenue. Programme cost: €500k. No loyalty value shown during booking. Price comparison against competitors (Ryanair/easyJet) on pure fare. No personalised upsell. Email capture optional and not incentivised.

✓ Solution

""Join CloudNine Rewards — get 500 bonus points + free priority boarding"" at booking confirmation. Bundled ancillaries with member pricing. Post-booking email capture with instant reward.

⚠ Pain Points

Communication void between booking and departure. No contextual ancillary offers. Baggage anxiety (size/weight confusion — see EU legal cases). No self-service options. This is where loyalty dies.

✓ Solution

6-message automated sequence with contextual offers. Mobile self-service hub (no app). Proactive disruption SMS with goodwill gestures. Points balance visible at every touchpoint.

In-flight experience is the only moment of truth — but for budget carriers, it's transactional. No loyalty touchpoint during the flight. No recognition of member status.

✓ Solution

Breeze Airways benchmark: staff ""niceness"" drove top Consumer Reports scores. Crew greeting for Frequent tier members. Post-landing SMS: ""Welcome to [city]. You earned X points.""

No post-trip engagement. No feedback collection. Points earned but invisible. No reason to return. 61% of 18-34s self-gift while holiday shopping — CloudNine captures none of this.

✓ Solution

T+7 email: ""You earned X points. Your balance: Y — enough for a free bag on your next trip."" Retargeting for Christmas self-gifters. Referral programme: give 500, get 500.
